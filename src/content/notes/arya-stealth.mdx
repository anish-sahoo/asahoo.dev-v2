title: the arya stealth, six months in

the arya stealth is the first pair of headphones i've owned where the cliché "they get out of the way" feels like a literal description rather than marketing. the soundstage is wider than the hd650's by a noticeable margin, separation between instruments is uncanny, and the treble has detail without ever crossing into the splashy territory that planars used to be infamous for. the stealth magnet revision really does seem to have tamed whatever was happening in the upper treble of older arya variants — i listen for hours without fatigue, which i couldn't say about the original arya i auditioned years ago.

the trade-offs are honest ones. the bass is fast and articulate but doesn't have the visceral midbass of the hd650, so big rock and electronic productions sound a little polite. they're also much more amplifier-fussy: paired with the fiio k11 r2r they sing, but plugged into a laptop they sound thin and a bit lifeless. for ambient, jazz, acoustic, and small-ensemble classical they're the best headphones i've owned at any price. for rap, edm, or a friday-night rock playlist, i still reach for the hd650.
